Old Photo Animation Guide

Old photo animation is about preserving memory, not creating wild motion. The safest path is a clean scan, gentle prompt, and short preview that keeps the original face and mood intact.

Animate an Old Photo

Best Old Photos

Clean scans

Scan or crop the photo so the face is clear and the image is not tilted or heavily damaged.

Visible face

Old portraits work better when eyes, mouth, and face outline are visible.

Gentle motion

Subtle blinking, a small smile, or a soft camera push-in usually feels more respectful and stable.

Prompt Examples

Family memory

Preserve identity, gentle blinking, subtle smile, warm nostalgic light, slow camera push-in.

Restored portrait

Keep original face and clothing unchanged, soft natural expression, minimal movement, no background changes.

What to Avoid

Avoid prompts that change age, clothing, pose, face shape, or background. For memory photos, the best result is often a quiet clip that feels alive without changing the person.
