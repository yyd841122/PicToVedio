How to Reduce AI Photo-to-Video Distortion

AI video is powerful, but it can bend faces, hands, pets, or products when the input photo is weak or the motion prompt is too aggressive. Start with stable inputs and simple movement.

Try a Stable AI Video Prompt

The Short Version

Use a clear photo

Sharp faces, visible products, and clean backgrounds give the model fewer things to invent.

Keep motion gentle

Prompts like slow camera push-in, subtle smile, or soft product spin usually work better than dramatic action.

Preview first

Run a short low-cost preview before spending credits on HD or no-watermark exports.

Best Prompt Patterns

Portraits and couples

Use: preserve identity, subtle smile, gentle head movement, natural eyes, soft cinematic light.

Products and pets

Use: clean camera push-in, product remains unchanged, pet stays natural, no object transformation.

When to Regenerate

If the result changes identity, adds unwanted objects, or creates strange hands or teeth, try a shorter clip, simpler prompt, or a clearer crop. For emotional videos, stable identity is usually more important than large motion.
